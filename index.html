Pico De Gallo

This salsa, also called Salsa Fresco and Salsa Cruda, is used as a dip served with tortilla chips and as a condiment with main dishes such as tacos and fajitas.

  • Time: 15 minutes
  • Serves: 4
Quantity Ingredients
5-6 ripe tomatoes, finely chopped
1 medium onion, finely chopped
2 cloves of garlic, minced or pressed
1/4 cup chopped cilantro
1/2 lime, juiced
1-2 serrano peppers, seeded and minced fine
salt and pepper to taste

Directions: Combine all ingredients in a bowl and refrigerate or serve at room temperature. Best when served fresh. Keeps in the refrigerator one week.

Tomatillo Salsa Verde

This salsa is excellent with pork and chicken. The tomatillo is a small green tomatoe found in the produce section of your grocery store. They come with a light brown husk which is removed before using.

  • Time: 45 minutes
  • Serves: 4
Quantity Ingredients
1 large onion, chopped
3 cloves of garlic, minced or pressed
3 Tbs olive oil
1 pound tomatillos, husks removed and chopped
1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro
1 jalapeno pepper, seeded and chopped fine
1 tsp sugar
1 tsp oregano
1 tsp basil
1 tsp ground cumin
1 tsp salt

Directions: Saute chopped onion and garlic in olive oil. Puree tomatillos in food processor or blender and add to onion and garlic. Simmer until soft and thickened. Add cilantro, jalapeno pepper, sugar and seasonings. Contiue cooking 10 minutes. Serve immediately.

Picante Sauce

Picante sauce is themost well known tomato salsa. It can be found bottled in your grocery store, but making it fresh is even better. This salsa can be used with just about any Tex-Mex dish. With a bowl full of tortilla chips it makes the easiest and best Tex-Mex appetizer.Keeps in the refrigerator one week.

  • Time: 45 minutes
  • Serves: 4
Quantity Ingredients
2 cloves of garlic, minced or pressed
1/2 medium onion, chopped
4 medium tomatoes, peeled and diced
1/4 green or red bell pepper, seeded and diced
1 tsp lime juice
1 jalapeno pepper, seeded and minced fine
salt to taste

Directions: Puree all ingredients in a food processor or blender, tryig to chop the ingredients without turning them into a liquid. Chop the ingredients by hand if you want a chuncky sytle sauce.

Place in a serving bowl and serve with tacos, tostadas, eggs, tortilla chips, or most any other Mexican dish. The uses of this salsa are limitless. Keeps in the refrigerator one week.
